Wretched Man That I Am
A Cry from the Furnace of Lust

“Wretched man that I am! Who will deliver me from this body of death?”(Romans 7:24) My flesh wars against my mind, And holds me captive to the law of sin(Romans 7:23). The desires of the flesh are against the Spirit(Galatians 5:17), And I do what I hate(Romans 7:15). I was enticed and carried away by my own lust(James 1:14), Sin conceived—and gave birth to death(James 1:15). I made a covenant with my eyes(Job 31:1), But my eyes made war with my soul(1 Peter 2:11). The flesh profits nothing(John 6:63), But still it whispers, “Taste and see.” Its path is wide, its gate is easy(Matthew 7:13), And many are those who walk it—including me. I cried aloud, but He did not answer(Lamentations 3:8), Because my iniquities have made a separation(Isaiah 59:2). I washed, but the stain remains(Jeremiah 2:22), The plague is within me(1 Kings 8:38). O God, create in me a clean heart(Psalm 51:10), Because mine is sick—deceitful above all things(Jeremiah 17:9). The law is holy, but I am sold under sin(Romans 7:12,14), I try to flee—but who will set me free? I know that nothing good dwells in me(Romans 7:18), Not in my flesh. Not in this war. I see the light, I long for truth— But sin lies crouching, and its desire is for me(Genesis 4:7). Deliver me, O God, from bloodguiltiness(Psalm 51:14), Open my lips, that my mouth may declare Your praise(Psalm 51:15). Draw me out of this pit(Psalm 40:2), Because I am drowning in a body that betrays me.
And yet, in the abyss of my soul’s torment, I cling to Your mercy, O Lord, for only You can shatter this furnace and make me whole.
